Ulriken

Information about Ulriken

Ulriken (but also known as Ålreken from the middel ages) is the tallest Mountian, among the seven mountain's that surround the city of Bergen. It has a height of 643 meters (2110 ft.) above the sea level. Ulriken does also have an aerial tram that transports tourists and hikers to the top of the moutain. At the top you could either visit the café that has an amazing viewpoint of Bergen, or you can plan out which trail you and your companions should hike.

The aerial tram

The tram "Perle & Bruse"

The aerail tram (goes by the name Ulriksbanen) that transport everything from tourists and hikers was finished built in 1961, but have also been through many upgrades since it's first trip. The gondola's were also named Perle and Bruse, the yellow one is called "Perle" and the red one is called "Bruse". The history comes from an artist called Carl Johan Skauge, the characters are based of two childeren that used to play around at Hansa Brewery

Hiking trails

Hiking

There are multiple trails you can take when you arrive at the top of Ulriken, they can either be short but fulfilling or long and refreshing. It's just up to you to decide what you want to experience, but you also have plan ahead of time so that the trip becomes enjoyable.
